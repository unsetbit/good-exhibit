Yuri Kochiyama, activist

Yuri Kochiyama fought for the rights of political prisoners, nuclear disarmerment, reparations for Japanese American internees, Native American sovereignty, Puerto Rican independence, and more. A close friend of Malcolm X, she held him on her lap as he died.

“Anybody getting arrested, no matter Black, Puerto Rican, or whatever, our first call was to her number. Her network was like no other. She would get a lawyer or get information out to our family and the Movement. You knew she wasn't going to stop until somebody heard from you.”

—Mutulu Shakur, black nationalist and Tupac's stepfather.

A Life of Caring

In her memoir, Yuri Kochiyama lists the following as some of the most significant moments in her life:

  • Meeting Malcolm X on October 16, 1963, and witnessing his assasination on February 21, 1965
  • Attending the Monroe, North Carolina hearing of Robert F. Williams after his return from China in 1968
  • Riding in a U-haul with ten citizens of the Republic of New Africa to a place they named El Malik in Mississippi in 1971
  • Attending the first Manzanar Pilgrimage in 1971
  • Participating in the 1977 take-over of the Statue of Liberty by Puerto Rican independentistas to demand the release of Andre Cordero, who was dying of cancer
  • Traveling to Cuba with the Venceremos Brigade in 1987
  • Traveling to Peru in 1993 on a human rights mission with Phil Farnham of the Revolutionary Communist Party
  • Traveling to the Philippines and Japan in 1994 to speak about the imprisonment of Abimael Guzman and the revolution in Peru
  • Having the opportunity to meet Mumia Abu Jamal in prison in Waynesburg, Pennsylvania
  • Attending the well organizaed Tule Lake Pilgrimage in fall 2002
