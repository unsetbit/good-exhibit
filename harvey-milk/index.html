Harvey Milk, politician

In January 1978, Harvey Milk became the first openly gay person to be elected to public office in California. He did not participate in activism or politics until his 40s, prior to which he was a public school teacher, an insurance actuary, a stock analyst, and a production associate for Broadway musicals.

In 2009, President Obama awarded him the Presidential Medal of Freedom for his work in the gay rights movement.

Stonewall Riots

On June 1969 a police raid at a gay bar in New York sparked six days of rioting by the gay rights community. The riots led to the first Gay Pride marches in U.S. history, which are now held annually throughout the world to commemorate the Stonewall riots.

White Night Riot

Harvey Milk and San Francisco Mayor George Moscone were assasinated in November of 1978 by Dan White. White was sentenced to only seven years in prison, sparking rioting.
