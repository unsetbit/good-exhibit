Your Privacy

XKeyscore is a U.S. government system for searching dragnet data which it collects from the internet, such as your emails or the fact that you're currently reading this text. This secret program was revealed in July 2013 by Edward Snowden and later confirmed by the U.S. government, which claimed that it is a “lawful foreign signals intelligence collection system”.

Many government surveillance programs, such as XKeyscore, don't distinguish between criminals and innocent people. It is up to you to protect your privacy by using the appropriate apps. The apps listed below are effective in protecting your data from being captured and inspected, whether by malicious hackers or government dragnets.

Private Messaging for iPhone

Signal is a free app for secure and private calls and messaging on iPhone. Signal is open-source project by Open Whisper Systems.

Private Messaging for Android

TextSecure is a free app for secure and private messaging on Android. TextSecure is also an open-source project by Open Whisper Systems.

Private Browsing

Tor is a free browser built by security researchers using concepts developed by the U.S. Naval Research Lab to secure communications online.

Hackers

“When I say I am a hacker, people often think I am making a naughty admission, presenting myself specifically as a security breaker. How did this confusion develop?

Around 1980, when the news media took notice of hackers, they fixated on one narrow aspect of real hacking: the security breaking which some hackers occasionally did. They ignored all the rest of hacking, and took the term to mean breaking security, no more and no less. The media have since spread that definition, disregarding our attempts to correct them. As a result, most people have a mistaken idea of what we hackers actually do and what we think.”

– Richard Stallman
